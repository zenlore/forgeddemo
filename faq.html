Frequently Asked Questions

What's a Macro?

Macro is slang for macronutrient. Macronutrients are broken up into three main categories: carbohydrates, fat, and protein. Macronutrients make up the majority of our diets, and our bodies rely on each macro category to function properly and efficiently.

How Do I Count My Macros?

Translating a macronutrient into something quantitative seems like it would be difficult. However, it's actually quite simple. Each macro category has a gram to calorie ratio:

  • 1 gram of protein = 4 calories
  • 1 gram of carbohydrate= 4 calories
  • 1 gram of fat = 9 calories

Each person has a daily macro goal made up of protein, carbs, and fats. For example, I assign you a macro allotment of 150 grams of protein, 200 grams of carbs, and 80 grams of fat per day. The goal is for you to consume this exact amount everyday. The idea of eating precise amounts of carbs, fat, and protein may seem fairly extreme at first, but in just a short amount of time macro counting will become a no brainer!

Macro tracking is made easy by purchasing MyMacros+ or MyFitnessPal through your iPhone or Android. Both apps have a wonderful database of pre entered foods and have easy to access options for entering items not found within the database.

What Am I Allowed To Eat?

This is the fun part! The beauty of flexible dieting and counting your macros is there are zero food restrictions, and you can eat whatever you please as long as you stay within your nutritional and micronutrient goals! This gives you the freedom to eat out with friends and keep your favorite ice cream around all while working towards your goals. I do not recommend spending all your macros on 'junk' foods, but thanks to flexible dieting you have the freedom to fit them into your meals on occasion.
